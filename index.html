Fieldwork & Outdoor Skills

Ecological restoration: Tree planting (pots and plugs), planting shrubs, moss, and grass, manual invasive plant removal, hiking, climbing, driving a 4x4, hitching trailers, (un)loading and carrying plants.
Camera trapping: Setting up, checking, and taking down camera traps; identifying species in photos; data cleaning and processing for images. (See more: fieldwork in the savanna)
Vegetation surveys: Estimating % cover, navigation with Trimble GPS and compass, tree height measurements with digital rangefinders.
Species ID: Plants in the Pacific Northwest, coastal and interior British Columbia; common mammals, birds, and fish in British Columbia; wildlife in African savannas.
Other experience from courses: Recording and analyzing hydrological data (e.g., stream discharge, flood frequency), analyzing soil profiles.
